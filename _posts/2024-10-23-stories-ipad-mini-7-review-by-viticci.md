> When I started looking at these products through this lens, I realized something. The iPad mini is the ideal third place device for things I don’t want to do on my iPhone or iPad Pro. By virtue of being so small, but bigger than a phone, it occupies a unique space in my digital life: it’s the place I go to when I want to read a book, browse the web, or watch some videos without having to be distracted by everything else that’s on my phone, or be reminded of the tasks I have to do on my iPad Pro. The iPad mini is, for me at least, pure digital escapism disguised as an 8.3” tablet.
> 
> From this perspective, I don’t need the iPad mini to run Stage Manager. I don’t need it to have a ProMotion display or more RAM. I don’t need it to be bigger or come with a Magic Keyboard. I need it, in fact, to be nothing more than it currently is. [I was wrong](https://mastodon.macstories.net/@viticci/113311771322032355) in trying to frame the iPad mini as an alternative to other models. The iPad mini would lose the fight in any comparison or measurement against the 11” iPad Pro.
> 
> 

Nelle scorse ore, insieme a quelle che vi ho menzionato qui, è arrivata anche la tanto desiderata review di Federico Viticci del nuovo iPad mini di settima generazione.

Come tanti altri, anche Federico rimane dell’idea che il prodotto in sé per sé fa il suo ed è un ottimo dispositivo multimediale. Certo l’assenza di Stage Manager si può far sentire, ma per chi?
